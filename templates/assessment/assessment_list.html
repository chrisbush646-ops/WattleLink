GRADE Evidence Certainty — Reference Guide

GRADE (Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development and Evaluations) is the international standard for rating certainty of evidence. Ratings reflect how confident we are that the estimated effect is close to the true effect. RCTs start at High; observational studies start at Low. Each concern in a domain typically downgrades certainty by one level.

High
We are very confident the true effect lies close to the estimate. Further research is very unlikely to change confidence.
Moderate
Moderately confident. Further research is likely to have an important impact and may change the estimate.
Low
Limited confidence. The true effect may be substantially different from the estimate of effect.
Very Low
Very little confidence. The true effect is likely substantially different from the estimate.
